## 3. 🤖 用 Claude + Z3/Python 解决国际象棋拼图
**All pieces on a 6 by 5 board**
- **来源**: John D. Cook
- **链接**: https://www.johndcook.com/blog/2026/06/20/z3-python-claude/
- **摘要**: John D. Cook 继续他的 LLM 代码生成实验系列：这次用 Claude 生成 Z3/Python 代码来解决一个经典棋盘拼图——在 6×5 棋盘上放置王、后、双象、双马、双车，要求双象在不同色格且无棋子互相攻击。Claude 生成的代码成功找到 192 个原始解，去重后得到 24 个唯一解。Cook 展示了完整的棋盘布局和 Z3 约束求解代码，这是 LLM 辅助形式化验证的又一成功案例。

> **原始正文：**
> I've written a couple posts lately on getting an LLM to generate code to solve chess problems. The first used Claude to generate Prolog and the second used ChatGPT to generate Prolog. This post will use Claude to generate Z3/Python code. The puzzle is one I've written about before: Place all the pieces—king, queen, two bishops, two knights, and two rooks—on a 6 × 5 chessboard, with the requirement that the two bishops be on opposite colored squares and no piece is attacking another. The code found 192 = 8 × 24 solutions. The factor of 8 comes from 2³ ways of swapping the pairs of bishops, knights, and rooks. The script reports 24 unique solutions after deduplicating piece-pair swaps.

## 7. 🎭 Jim Nielsen：满页瘫痪
**Full Page Paralysis**
- **来源**: Jim Nielsen's Blog
- **链接**: https://blog.jim-nielsen.com/2026/full-page-paralysis/
- **摘要**: Jim Nielsen 写了一篇短小精悍的感悟：人们常说的「空白页瘫痪」（blank page paralysis）其实不是最难的部分——完成才是。在软件里叫「最后 90%」，在物流里叫「最后一英里」。当一件作品接近完成时，内心会有个声音说：「只要还没完成，它就没有缺点，它仍然是完美的可能。」Nielsen 坦言自己不怕开始，但在面对一整页即将发布的内容时会陷入瘫痪。

> **原始正文：**
> You've probably heard the term. It's meant to convey how difficult it can be to start something. "Blank page paralysis". But for my money, beginning is easy. Finishing is the hard part. In software, they call it "the last 90%". In logistics, they call it "the last mile". It's that final stretch that's disproportionately hard. Finishing makes something real and finite, subject to judgment. As I near completion, there's a little voice in my head that says, "As long as it's unfinished, there's nothing wrong with it. It's still potentially perfect!" I don't struggle with blank page paralysis. But I am paralyzed in the face of a full page ready for publishing.

## 10. 🌍 Ada Palmer 论文艺复兴：印刷术、审查与信息革命
**Inventing the Renaissance — Dwarkesh Interview with Ada Palmer**
- **来源**: Dwarkesh Podcast
- **链接**: https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/ada-palmer
- **摘要**: 历史学家 Ada Palmer 在 Dwarkesh 播客中深入讨论了文艺复兴时期的信息革命。核心洞见包括：羊皮纸时代一本手抄本的成本相当于一栋房子；纸莎草的消失导致了古典时代大部分知识的丧失——不是亚历山大图书馆被烧，而是 400-600 年间纸莎草脆化碎裂。宗教裁判所意外发明了同行评审——他们在审查书籍时需要复现实验来验证真伪。每个时代对什么值得审查总是错的：宗教裁判所专注于细微的神学异端，却忽视了马基雅维利和卢克莱修。

> **原始正文：**
> Ada Palmer discusses the information revolution of the Renaissance on the Dwarkesh podcast. Key insights: In the parchment age, a handwritten medieval book cost as much as a house. The loss of papyrus caused most of classical knowledge to disappear — not at the burning of the Library of Alexandria, but between 400-600 AD when papyri became brittle and crumbled. Monks had to choose which books to save, and their subjective tastes became an unintentional censorship. The Inquisition accidentally invented peer review — they needed to reproduce experiments to verify the books they were censoring. Every era is always wrong about what ideas and changes are the truly big ones: the Inquisition was obsessed with minor Protestant theological disputes while ignoring Machiavelli and Lucretius.
