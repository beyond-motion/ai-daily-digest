## 1. 🤖 微软新模型 MAI
**Microsoft's new MAI models**
- **来源**: Simon Willison's Weblog
- **链接**: https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/2/microsofts-new-models/#atom-everything
- **摘要**: 微软发布两款新 LLM：MAI-Thinking-1（推理模型，35B 参数）和 MAI-Code-1-Flash（5B 参数，专为 GitHub Copilot 和 VS Code 打造）。值得注意的是，微软声称这些模型使用"企业级、清洁且商业许可的数据"训练，未从第三方模型蒸馏——如果属实，这可能是首批不依赖未授权网络数据训练的通用代码专用模型。

> **原始正文：**
> Microsoft announced two new text LLMs this morning - MAI-Thinking-1 (reasoning, 35B parameters, available to "select early partners") and MAI-Code-1-Flash (5B parameters, "purpose-built for GitHub Copilot and VS Code to deliver high performance and lower cost [...] rolling out to GitHub Copilot individual users in Visual Studio Code"). I've not been able to try either of them just yet. It's very interesting to see Microsoft releasing models with such low parameter counts, especially given how expensive larger models are to access right now. They claim MAI-Thinking-1 "is preferred to Sonnet 4.6 in our blind human side-by-side evaluations", which is impressive for a 35B model seeing as I frequently run models larger than that on my own laptop. Also of note: We trained [MAI-Thinking-1] from the ground up on enterprise grade, clean and commercially licensed data, without distillation from third-party models. And for MAI-Code-1-Flash as well: It is built end-to-end by Microsoft using clean and appropriately licensed data. I would very much like to learn more about this "appropriately licensed" data! Could these be the first generally useful code-specialist models that didn't train on an unlicensed dump of the web?

## 8. 🎨 计算机的精确刻板
**An Ode to the Exacting Pedantry of Computers**
- **来源**: Jim Nielsen's Blog
- **链接**: https://blog.jim-nielsen.com/2026/pedantry-of-computing/
- **摘要**: Jim Nielsen 回忆从 C++ 到 Python 的编程入门经历，反思计算机的"精确刻板"本质：程序只做你告诉它做的事，bug 是你想象力的失败而非执行的失败。而如今 LLM 改变了这一切——"按我的意思做，不是按我说的做"终于成为可能。

> **原始正文：**
> The very first computer programming class I ever took introduced me to the idea of there being different kinds of numbers, like integers, floats, and doubles. A few years later, Python felt like the computer was meeting me partway. Over time, I came to learn how pedantic computers are. They require a kind of exacting precision. A "bug" was very likely a flaw in your conception of how the program should execute, not the actual execution. It was a failure on your part to be more precise. "Do what I mean, not what I say!" But now, with LLMs, that kind of exacting precision is no longer required — and that's a fundamental shift in how we interact with computers.

## 10. 🕰️ Cyrix 486DLC：1992年的历史回声
**Cyrix 486DLC CPU: Introduced June 1992**
- **来源**: The Silicon Underground / Dave Farquhar
- **链接**: https://dfarq.homeip.net/cyrix-486dlc-cpu-introduced-june-1992/
- **摘要**: 1992年6月第一周，Cyrix 推出 486DLC CPU。Cyrix 没有自己的晶圆厂，与德州仪器达成制造协议，TI 也可以销售该芯片。这段历史映射了当今芯片行业 fabless 模式的早期雏形。

> **原始正文：**
> In the first week of June 1992, Cyrix debuted its 486DLC CPU. Cyrix didn't have its own fabrication plants so they made arrangements with Texas Instruments to manufacture the chips in May 1992. Part of the agreement allowed TI to sell the chips under their own branding as well. This was an early example of the fabless semiconductor model that would later become standard in the industry.
